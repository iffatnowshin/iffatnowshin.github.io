This project focuses on enhancing the efficiency of photovoltaic (PV) modules by mitigating the adverse effects of soiling and dust accumulation. By leveraging advanced machine learning frameworks, particularly Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and VGG16 architectures, we developed robust automated dust detection systems.

The core of this initiative involves combining these detection algorithms with IoT-based automated water sprinkle systems to create a responsive and efficient cleaning strategy. Furthermore, a novel transmittance distribution approach was introduced to precisely quantify the soiling effects on the performance of the PV modules.

The outcomes of this project contribute significantly to the predictive maintenance of solar panels, ensuring optimal energy harvesting even in environments prone to heavy dust accumulation.

### Key Contributions

- Development of CNN and VGG16 based models for accurate dust detection on PV panels.
- Implementation of an IoT-based automated water sprinkle cleaning mechanism.
- Introduction of a novel transmittance distribution approach to quantify soiling effects.
