With the growing need for sustainable and reliable energy sources, particularly in the South Asian context, this project explores the techno-economic feasibility of hybrid renewable energy systems. We specifically analyzed the integration of Solar, Wind, and Biogas energy resources in Bangladesh.

By employing advanced metaheuristic optimization algorithms, the project evaluated various configurations of these hybrid systems to minimize the Levelized Cost of Energy (LCOE) while ensuring high reliability (reducing the Loss of Power Supply Probability - LPSP).

This comprehensive modeling provides valuable insights for policymakers and energy planners aiming to transition towards cleaner energy grids in developing regions.

### Highlights

- Comprehensive techno-economic analysis of Solar/Wind/Biogas hybrid systems.
- Application of state-of-the-art metaheuristic algorithms for system optimization.
- Contextual focus on the energy landscape of Bangladesh and the broader South Asian region.
